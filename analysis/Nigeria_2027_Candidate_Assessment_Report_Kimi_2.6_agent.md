## THE INTEGRITY PARADOX

This assessment reveals a devastating structural pattern in Nigeria's 2027 presidential field:

| Political Acumen Score | Integrity Score | Candidate |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| 8 | 2 | Atiku Abubakar |
| 8 | 1 | Bola Tinubu |
| 6 | 4 | Peter Obi |
| 5 | 5 | Seyi Makinde |
| 5 | 4 | Goodluck Jonathan |
| 3 | 2 | Rabiu Kwankwaso |

**The paradox:** The two candidates with the highest political skill (Atiku and Tinubu, both scoring 8) have the lowest integrity scores (2 and 1 respectively). Conversely, the candidate with the best administrative record (Obi, scoring 8) has a compromised integrity score of 4 due to Pandora Papers and human rights abuses. Only Seyi Makinde achieves a moderate balance (5 across political, administrative, and integrity dimensions), but without the transformative track record of Obi or the political machinery of Atiku/Tinubu.

This creates an impossible electoral choice for Nigerian voters: **competence without integrity, or integrity without sufficient political machinery to govern effectively.**

## SCORING METHODOLOGY

### Framework Source
The 5-Measurable-Terms Framework is drawn from political science, leadership studies, public administration, and ethics scholarship (Greenstein 2000; Northouse 2016; Lee & Kim 2018; Nawaz et al. 2023; Mozumder 2021). Each dimension is operationalized through qualitative and quantitative indicators.

### Scoring Process
1. **Evidence Extraction:** Every claim in each candidate's PDF was catalogued as positive (+) or negative (-) within each dimension.
2. **Weighting Within Dimensions:** Positive and negative indicators were weighed against each other based on severity, documentation quality, and institutional verification.
3. **Scale Calibration:** 0 = No evidence of competence / overwhelming evidence of failure; 5 = Moderate performance with significant flaws; 10 = Exceptional performance with minimal flaws.
4. **No candidate scored 9 or 10** in any dimension because no candidate's record is without material controversy.

### Weighting Scenarios
- **Equal Weight (20% each):** All five dimensions treated as equally important.
- **Integrity-Weighted (30% Integrity, 17.5% others):** Reflects the framework's emphasis that integrity is "the most vital quality" and "most consistently linked to long-term legitimacy."

### Fitness Thresholds
| Status | Criteria |
|:---|:---|
| **STRONGLY FIT** | > 6.5 overall AND Integrity >= 4 |
| **FIT** | 5.5 - 6.5 overall AND Integrity >= 4 |
| **MARGINAL** | 5.0 - 5.4 overall OR Integrity < 4 |
| **NOT FIT** | < 5.0 overall |

## KEY FINDINGS & IMPLICATIONS

### 1. No Candidate is Fully Qualified
The highest score is 6.8/10 (Peter Obi, equal weight). No candidate approaches the 8.0+ threshold that would indicate strong fitness for high office. This is not a failure of the framework but an accurate reflection of a political field where every contender carries significant documented baggage.

### 2. Integrity is the Decisive Differentiator
When integrity is weighted at 30%, the rankings shift meaningfully:
- Tinubu drops from tied 3rd to 4th (integrity score of 1 is catastrophic)
- Jonathan rises from 5th to 5th (integrity score of 4 is his saving grace)
- The gap between Obi (6.45) and Tinubu (5.20) widens to 1.25 points

### 3. The "Experience vs. Cleanliness" Trade-off
- **Most experienced:** Atiku (VP 1999-2007, 30 years in politics) and Tinubu (Governor 1999-2007, President 2023-present)
- **Cleanest record:** Makinde (no major international scandal, transparent asset declarations)
- **Best balance:** Obi (strong administrative record + moderate integrity compromise)

### 4. Kwankwaso is Structurally Eliminated
With scores of 5 (Communication), 6 (Vision), 5 (Admin), 3 (Political), and 2 (Integrity), Kwankwaso fails on multiple fronts. The loss of NNPP legal recognition, the defection of his political godson to APC, and the pending U.S. sanctions bill render him non-viable.

### 5. Tinubu's Incumbency Advantage is Not Reflected in Scores
The framework measures fitness for leadership, not electoral probability. Tinubu's control of state machinery, federal treasury, and security apparatus gives him overwhelming structural advantages not captured in these scores. He may win re-election while being the least fit candidate ethically.
